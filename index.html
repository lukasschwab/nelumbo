Berkeley, California

We use a thin superomniphobic nanocoating on air conditioning cooling coil fins to accelerate water and oil shedding, eliminating the common issues of decreased energy efficiency and contaminant build-up.


Our Omniphobe™ coating is thermally conductive, 200x thinner than a human hair, and costs less than 2% of an AC unit.


That means universal application on any scale or geometry.

Recognition

First Place, Berkeley Cleantech University Prize

Sponsored by the U.S. Department of Energy and hosted by UC Berkeley, the CUP program provided selected teams with 15 weeks of cleantech entrepreneurial training, culminating in a pitch event for a panel of judges including D.O.E. representatives, Berkeley Haas Business School affiliates, and Clean Technology and Energy Venture Capitalists. Nelumbo was selected from seven teams for the grand prize $100,000 grant and advancement to the national finals, scheduled for June 2016.


Current Finalist, Cleantech to Market

Cleantech to Market (C2M) is a partnership between students, scientists, engineers, and professionals to translate cleantech research, green chemistry, and water research into market opportunities. In the process, C2M helps develop the next generation of innovative cleantech leaders. Nelumbo has been selected for the Fall 2016 C2M program, and will recieve up to 1000 hours of free market research from Berkeley Haas MBA students.
